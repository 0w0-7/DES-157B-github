The

Nursing Shortage

As the average age of our population rises, the demand for nurses increases. However, there is a shortage in the supply of nurses to meet this growing need.

Involved in a car accident and sustained a head injury. John initially suffers a mild concussion from the car accident, leading to symptoms of a mild headache, dizziness, and slight confusion.

Initial Check-In Process

John walked into the ER, where the front desk was overwhelmed with waiting patients.

After checking in at the front desk, he was told to take a seat.

Despite his visible anxiety, John begrudgingly took a seat and proceeded to wait half an hour.

Extended Waiting Period

Medical Distress

After having now waited an hour he started experiencing severe headaches.

John's condition continued to deteriorate as he sat waiting.

He suddenly began convulsing, having a seizure in the waiting area.

Bystanders allerted Medical staff immediately.

Immediate Medical Attention

Stabilization Efforts

The medical team quickly responded to the emergency and was assigned a level 1: high priority patient.

He was placed on a stretcher and rushed into a private room where initial assessments and stabilizing treatments were initiated.

Vital signs were monitored closely, and necessary medications were administered.

John's condition remained critical despite efforts to improve his state of being.

Cardiac Arrest

Resuscitation Efforts

John's heart rate dropped dramatically and resuscitation efforts were initiated immediately.

Life support measures were performed. Multiple rounds of CPR and defibrillation were carried out.

Despite the efforts to save John given the nurse’s working conditions, there was a lack of timely medical attention.

John was unable to be revived and was pronounced dead after prolonged resuscitation attempts.

Was the care adequate?

How can the nurse adequately care for John while neglecting their own well-being, taking on extra shifts and patients due to staffing shortages?

Many potential issues could have been overlooked as nurses aided John, therefore impacting the possibility of his survival.

Statistics

US Population 65 Years and Over

In 2020, about 1 in 6 people in the United States were age 65 and over. In 1920, this proportion was less than 1 in 20.

Largest 10 year numeric gain, 2010-2020 had an increase of
15 million people, totaling 55.8 million.

As people age, demand for complex care increases, particularly for managing various chronic conditions.

Additional Factors

Retirement

By 2030, over 1 million nurses are expected to retire, with a majority of registered nurses being 50 years old or older, and the average age of RNs being 52.

School Enrollment

Nursing school enrollment is not growing fast enough to meet the projected demand.

Majority Women

With over 86% of nurses being women, many typically exit the workforce during childbearing phases, with some returning later while others pursue different careers.

Nurse Bullying

Ongoing bullying and violence among nurses in healthcare settings may deter both current and prospective candidates from pursuing careers in nursing.

Faculty Shortage

The shortage of nursing faculty directly limits the yearly enrollment of future nurses and RNs. In 2021-2022, almost 92,000 qualified applicants were turned away from nursing programs due to faculty shortages and insufficient resources, reports the American Association of Colleges of Nursing.
